NGO's List

Goonj

Goonj is an NGO based in India that addresses issues like poverty, social inequality, and urban-rural divides through sustainable initiatives. Founded in 1999 by Anshu Gupta, Goonj repurposes urban surplus materials, particularly clothing, to meet the basic needs of marginalized communities.Its key programs include the "Cloth for Work" initiative, where villagers exchange their labor for essential goods, and rural development projects like building roads, sanitation systems, and improving infrastructure. Goonj also provides disaster relief, educational campaigns, and raises awareness about resource management. Users of a donation platform can contribute financially, donate old clothes and goods, volunteer, or participate in fundraising events..

Donation Type: Clothes, Electronics, Others

HelpAge India

HelpAge India is a nonprofit organization focused on supporting the elderly in India, addressing issues such as poverty, health challenges, and social isolation. Founded in 1978, it provides healthcare services, financial assistance, and advocacy for the rights of senior citizens. Key programs include mobile healthcare units, old-age pensions, and caregiver support. HelpAge India often accepts clothing donations for senior citizens, providing them with essential items. The organization also works on legal advocacy to protect the rights of older adults.HelpAge India also focuses on empowering communities to better care for their elderly members. It conducts workshops and training for caregivers to improve the quality of elderly care at home, ensuring that seniors receive proper attention and support. In the event of natural disasters, the organization provides emergency relief, ensuring that senior citizens are not left behind in disaster response efforts.

Donation Type: Clothes

Smile Foundation

Smile Foundation is an NGO in India focused on empowering underprivileged children, youth, and women through education, healthcare, and livelihood programs. Founded in 2002, it aims to address critical issues like poverty, education, and health, particularly for marginalized communities. The foundation runs several initiatives, including Mission Education for providing quality education, Smile on Wheels for mobile healthcare, and Smile Twin e-Learning Program for digital education. It also promotes women empowerment through skill development and health awareness programs. Users can contribute to Smile Foundation by making financial donations, donating materials like clothing and books, volunteering, or participating in fundraising campaigns. Information on how to donate is available on their website and social media platforms, where the foundation shares updates on its work and impact. Supporting Smile Foundation helps provide essential resources to those in need, especially in education and healthcare.

Donation Type: Clothes, Money, Books

Teach for India

Teach for India is a nonprofit organization that aims to eliminate educational inequity in India by recruiting and training young professionals to teach in underprivileged schools. Founded in 2009, it is part of the global Teach for All network. The organization focuses on providing quality education to children from low-income communities, empowering them to reach their full potential. Teach for India fellows, who are typically graduates or young professionals, work in schools across various cities for two years, serving as teachers and leaders in the classroom. They also engage with the community to foster long-term change in education. The initiative addresses the systemic gaps in the Indian education system by combining classroom teaching with leadership development, advocating for the importance of education in social change. Teach for India also encourages corporate and individual donations, volunteer opportunities, and partnerships to support its mission and extend its impact..

Donation Type: Money, Books
